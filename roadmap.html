Build Your Employability

Everything you can do during your degree to build the experience, skills and confidence employers look for — alongside the careers support available to you at Liverpool Hope.

Wider than you think

Psychology graduates work across a far broader range of careers than most students realise — from HR, behavioural science and research to marketing, public services, digital and education. The career pathways on this site map where graduates actually go, not just the obvious routes.

The gap employers identify

Employers consistently say the gap in graduate CVs is not academic knowledge — it is practical work readiness and relevant experience. The activities on this page are how you start closing that gap during your degree, regardless of which career direction you are heading in.

You do not need to have your career planned out to start building employability. The activities below develop skills that employers value across all psychology career pathways.

Getting experience as a psychology student

Build Experience While You Study

Experience does not need to be perfectly matched to your plans to be valuable. The key is developing the skills employers look for — communication, research, empathy, analytical thinking, professionalism — through whatever is available to you.

Research Experience

Research skills are one of psychology's strongest selling points with employers — and they are easy to underestimate.

  • Undergraduate research assistant roles within the School of Psychology
  • Staff-led research projects — ask your lecturers directly
  • Choose an applied dissertation topic relevant to your career interests
  • Build data skills through SPSS, R, or Excel online courses

People-Facing Experience

Demonstrates the empathy, communication and professional boundaries employers expect from psychology graduates.

  • Peer mentoring within Liverpool Hope
  • Volunteering with community or charity organisations
  • Support worker or care roles (part-time)
  • Coaching, tutoring, or youth work

Liverpool Hope Careers Support

Liverpool Hope's careers service offers one-to-one appointments, employer events, and funded opportunities throughout your degree — and for up to four years after graduation.

Whether you are interested in clinical training, HR, research, or something you have not yet considered, a careers appointment is the best starting point. Standing out to employers

Four Things That Make a Real Difference

These are the most consistently impactful things psychology students can do to improve their employment outcomes — practical, actionable, and possible to start now.

Using LinkedIn

LinkedIn is one of the most useful tools for exploring career pathways, connecting with alumni and building a professional profile early.

  • Set up a profile with your degree, university and a short summary of your interests
  • Follow Liverpool Hope University and relevant employers
  • Connect with psychology graduates to learn about their career paths
  • Use the Jobs section to explore what graduate roles actually look like
  • Engage with content from employers and the BPS

CV Guidance

Your degree builds skills employers value — the challenge is describing them clearly and evidencing them with specific examples.

  • Frame research methods as data collection, analysis and report writing
  • Describe presentations and group projects as communication and teamwork evidence
  • Include part-time work, volunteering and student roles — they all count
  • Tailor your personal statement to each role you apply for
  • Book a one-to-one session with a careers adviser for feedback
Book CV support →

Volunteering and Community Involvement

Any role involving working with people or gathering information builds relevant skills for a psychology career.

  • Support, care and welfare roles in community settings
  • Charities working with mental health, youth, or social issues
  • School or educational volunteering
  • Research or data work for community organisations

Get Involved at University

Activities within university life build leadership, communication and teamwork skills that employers consistently rank as most important — and they are easy to start.

  • Student Ambassador Scheme — builds professional communication
  • Student Representative roles — demonstrates initiative and leadership
  • Psychology Society committee — teamwork and event management
  • BPS Student Membership — begins building your professional identity
  • Peer mentoring — people skills and professional boundaries
