20,300 psychology graduates complete their degrees each year. Around 12,000 go on to work in psychology-related careers, while approximately 1,300 become registered psychologists. Although many students begin their degree hoping to work in mental health, psychology graduates build successful careers across a far wider range of sectors.

Importantly, 59% of psychology graduates consider their current job to be in the field of psychology — whether or not they are working in a registered role. This site is here to help you understand the full range of careers your psychology degree opens up, and to build the experience, skills and confidence to get there.

Getting experience and support

Build Your Employability

Evidence consistently shows that work experience and careers support are among the most decisive factors in graduate employment outcomes. Here is how you can start building yours now.

Build Experience While You Study

Research experience, people-facing roles, leadership activities and placements — each builds skills that employers across all 11 career pathways consistently look for.

  • Undergraduate research assistant roles
  • Support work, peer mentoring, volunteering
  • Student Ambassador and Representative roles
  • Optional placement year module
Liverpool Hope Careers Support

Free, confidential careers advice tailored to psychology students — available throughout your degree and for up to four years after graduation.

  • One-to-one careers appointments
  • Employer events and campus talks
  • Student Futures paid internships
  • Reach Your Full Potential programme

After you graduate

What Happens After Graduation

Your degree is a starting point. Most psychology career paths begin with a graduate-entry role that builds experience — some routes require further training to reach practitioner status.

Graduate entry

Direct entry roles

HR, research, marketing, public sector, charity and consultancy careers are open on graduation with no further training required.

Further training

Postgraduate routes

Clinical, forensic, occupational and sport psychology require a BPS-accredited Masters or doctorate. Experience during your degree matters significantly.

Graduate entry

Graduate schemes

NHS, Civil Service, retail, finance, consultancy and more run schemes open to psychology graduates. Most open in autumn of your final year.

Ongoing

Alumni support

Liverpool Hope's careers service is available to graduates for up to four years after completing your degree.
