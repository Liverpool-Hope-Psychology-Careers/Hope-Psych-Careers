People, Organisations and Consultancy

HR, occupational psychology, management consultancy and organisational development — careers where psychology graduates apply their understanding of people and organisations.

Psychology graduates are well suited to roles that involve understanding human behaviour in workplace settings. This theme covers careers in HR, people management, occupational psychology, and consultancy, where knowledge of motivation, communication, behaviour change, and group dynamics is directly applicable.

What you could do now
  • Take on a committee or leadership role in a student society
  • Look for part-time work in a people-facing role such as retail, hospitality or customer service
  • Explore CIPD student membership and resources
  • Attend careers events focused on HR, business, or graduate schemes
  • Look into the Civil Service Fast Stream and large graduate employer schemes

Human Resources

HR is one of the most common destinations for psychology graduates. Knowledge of recruitment, motivation, learning, and organisational behaviour translates directly into roles that manage, develop, and support people at work.

Why Psychology?
  • Understanding of what motivates people and how to get the best from individuals and teams
  • Knowledge of learning theory and behaviour change, directly applicable to training and development
  • Research and analytical skills for measuring performance and evaluating HR initiatives
  • Strong communication and interpersonal skills developed throughout the degree
  • Understanding of group dynamics, conflict, and organisational behaviour
Possible roles in this area
Recruitment Consultant / Talent Acquisition
Uses psychological knowledge to assess candidates, design interviews, and identify talent for organisations.
Graduate entry
Learning and Development Officer
Designs and delivers training programmes using principles of learning, behaviour change, and motivation.
Graduate entry
Organisational Development Adviser
Applies psychology to improve workplace culture, team dynamics, and organisational effectiveness.
Graduate entry
HR Graduate Scheme
Structured graduate programmes at large employers that rotate across HR functions including recruitment, reward, and employee relations.
Graduate entry
Skills that may be useful
Communication Empathy and active listening Understanding of motivation Data and analytical skills Organisation and planning Ethical awareness
Example employers
  • Large graduate employers including Deloitte, PwC, Unilever and the NHS
  • Local authorities and public sector organisations
  • Recruitment agencies and headhunting firms
  • HR consultancies and people management firms
What you could do now
  • Explore CIPD student membership and free resources on HR careers
  • Take on a people-facing role in part-time work to build communication and interpersonal skills
  • Research large graduate HR schemes and their application timelines
  • Attend careers fairs and employer events focused on business and HR

People Management

Psychology graduates bring strong interpersonal skills and an understanding of motivation and communication to management roles, making them effective leaders at all levels of an organisation.

Why Psychology?
  • Deep understanding of what motivates individuals and how to bring out the best in people
  • Knowledge of group dynamics, conflict resolution, and team behaviour
  • Strong communication skills, including the ability to adapt style to different audiences
  • Awareness of wellbeing and mental health in the workplace
  • Analytical thinking and evidence-based decision making
Possible roles in this area
Team Leader / Line Manager
Entry-level management positions overseeing small teams, drawing on interpersonal and communication skills.
Graduate entry
Graduate Management Scheme
Structured programmes at large organisations that develop future managers and leaders across different business areas.
Graduate entry
Leadership Development Coordinator
Supports the identification and development of leadership talent within an organisation, often sitting within HR or L&D.
Graduate entry
Skills that may be useful
Leadership and influence Communication Motivation and behaviour Conflict resolution Decision making Emotional intelligence
Example employers
  • Retail and hospitality chains with graduate management programmes
  • NHS and public sector management schemes
  • Large graduate employers including Marks & Spencer, John Lewis, and Tesco
  • Charities and third sector organisations with management roles
What you could do now
  • Take on a leadership or committee role in a student society
  • Apply for a student ambassador or peer mentor role
  • Seek out part-time work in a team environment and aim for a supervisor role
  • Research graduate management schemes and their requirements

Occupational Psychology

Occupational psychology applies psychological research to the world of work, focusing on improving performance, wellbeing, and organisational effectiveness. With further training, it leads to chartered practitioner status — one of the seven protected titles in psychology.

Why Psychology?
  • Occupational psychology is built directly on undergraduate psychology — it applies theories of motivation, learning, and behaviour in workplace settings
  • Research and statistical skills are central to assessment, evaluation, and evidence-based practice
  • Understanding of wellbeing, stress, and mental health is increasingly valued by employers
  • Knowledge of group behaviour, leadership, and organisational change informs consultancy and advisory work
Possible roles in this area
Wellbeing at Work Coordinator
Designs and implements workplace wellbeing initiatives drawing on psychological research and behaviour change principles.
Graduate entry
Employee Engagement Analyst
Uses surveys, data, and psychological insight to measure and improve employee motivation and job satisfaction.
Graduate entry
Chartered Occupational Psychologist
A fully qualified practitioner, registered with the HCPC, working across areas including assessment, selection, coaching, organisational development, and wellbeing.
Postgrad training required
Skills that may be useful
Psychological theory Research and evaluation Data analysis Communication and presentation Ethical practice Problem solving
Example employers
  • Occupational psychology consultancies and HR advisory firms
  • NHS and public sector organisations with OD or workforce functions
  • Large corporations with in-house people analytics or wellbeing teams
  • Assessment and selection companies
What you could do now
  • Research BPS-accredited MSc programmes in Occupational Psychology
  • Explore the BPS Division of Occupational Psychology and the Association for Business Psychology
  • Look for assistant or analyst roles in HR, wellbeing, or people teams as a graduate entry point
  • Build data and research skills through your dissertation or online courses

Consultancy

Consultancy roles allow psychology graduates to apply their analytical and people skills to help organisations solve problems and improve performance, often working across a range of clients and sectors.

Why Psychology?
  • Analytical and critical thinking skills developed through research methods and statistics
  • Understanding of human behaviour, motivation, and decision-making — central to advising organisations
  • Strong written and verbal communication skills for presenting findings and recommendations
  • Knowledge of behaviour change and organisational dynamics
  • Ability to synthesise evidence and translate it into practical recommendations
Possible roles in this area
Management Consultant
Advises organisations on strategy, structure, and performance, drawing on analytical and communication skills. Many large firms run psychology-friendly graduate schemes.
Graduate entry
Change Management Consultant
Manages the human side of organisational change, drawing on psychological knowledge of behaviour, motivation, and resistance to change.
Graduate entry
Behavioural Science Consultant
Applies behavioural science and psychological research to help organisations understand and influence the behaviour of employees, customers, or users.
Graduate entry
Learning Experience Designer
Designs engaging learning and development programmes for corporate or educational settings, drawing on psychology of learning and instructional design principles.
Graduate entry
Skills that may be useful
Analytical thinking Communication and presentation Problem solving Understanding of behaviour Report writing Client management
Example employers
  • Big Four firms — Deloitte, PwC, KPMG, EY — all run graduate schemes open to psychology graduates
  • Specialist behavioural science consultancies such as the Behavioural Insights Team
  • HR and organisational development consultancies
  • Public sector and government advisory bodies
What you could do now
  • Research graduate consultancy schemes — many are open to all degree subjects
  • Build analytical and presentation skills through your degree assessments
  • Look into the Behavioural Insights Team and similar organisations for internship opportunities
  • Practice case study interviews, which are commonly used in consultancy recruitment
