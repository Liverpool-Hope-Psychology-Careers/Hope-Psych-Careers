Research, Data and Evaluation

Research design, data analysis and programme evaluation — careers where the research skills at the heart of a psychology degree are directly applied.

The research training at the heart of a psychology degree provides strong foundations for careers in academic and applied research, data analysis, and programme evaluation. Psychology graduates are skilled at designing studies, analysing data, and communicating findings clearly — all highly valued across a wide range of sectors.

What you could do now
  • Approach academic staff about volunteering as a research assistant — many projects need help with data collection and analysis
  • Build your statistical skills through your degree and consider learning R, SPSS or Excel beyond what is required
  • Access free datasets through the UK Data Service or Kaggle to build a data portfolio
  • Apply for a Research Assistant role during or after your degree
  • Attend research seminars and conferences to understand how psychology research is conducted and communicated

Academic Research

Academic research roles allow you to contribute directly to the development of psychological knowledge. Research Assistant positions are often available during undergraduate study, making this one of the most accessible areas to get started in.

Why Psychology?
  • In-depth training in research design, methodology, and statistical analysis throughout the degree
  • Experience of conducting and writing up original research through assessments and the dissertation
  • Understanding of psychological theory that informs and contextualises research questions
  • Critical thinking and evidence evaluation skills essential for academic work
  • Familiarity with ethical considerations in research with human participants
Possible roles in this area
Research Assistant
Supports academic researchers with data collection, transcription, analysis, and literature reviews. Often available as a paid or voluntary role during undergraduate study.
Available during degree
Postgraduate Researcher (PhD)
Conducts original doctoral research in psychology or an adjacent field, leading to an academic or applied research career.
Postgrad training required
Academic Lecturer / Researcher
Teaches and conducts research at university level, typically requiring a PhD and a strong publication record.
PhD required
Skills that may be useful
Research design Statistical analysis Academic writing Critical thinking Attention to detail Ethical awareness
Example employers
  • Universities and higher education institutions across the UK
  • Research institutes and think tanks
  • NHS research departments and clinical trials units
  • Government research bodies including the Economic and Social Research Council (ESRC)
What you could do now
  • Ask academic staff in the School of Psychology about Research Assistant opportunities
  • Put effort into your dissertation — it is often the strongest piece of evidence of research ability
  • Attend research seminars and departmental events to understand academic research culture
  • If considering a PhD, look into ESRC studentships and research council funding routes

Applied Research

Applied research roles use psychological methods to address real-world questions in business, policy, and the public sector. These careers are a great fit for psychology graduates who enjoy research but want to see their work make a direct practical difference.

Why Psychology?
  • Psychology graduates are trained in both quantitative and qualitative research methods — a rare combination valued in applied settings
  • Understanding of human behaviour, attitudes, and decision-making informs research design and interpretation
  • Skills in survey design, interviewing, and data analysis translate directly into market and policy research roles
  • Ability to communicate research findings clearly to non-specialist audiences
  • Ethical awareness around data collection and participant rights is increasingly valued by employers
Possible roles in this area
Market Research Executive
Designs and conducts research to understand consumer behaviour, attitudes, and preferences for commercial clients.
Graduate entry
Policy Researcher
Uses psychological and social research methods to generate evidence that informs government or third sector policy decisions.
Graduate entry
Social Researcher (Government)
Conducts research for government departments to inform policy, service design, and public communications.
Graduate entry
Consumer Insights Analyst
Analyses data and research findings to understand how and why consumers make decisions, informing business strategy.
Graduate entry

Quantitative and qualitative methods Survey design Data interpretation Report writing Communication Project management

  • Government Social Research profession across central government departments
  • Think tanks and policy organisations such as the Nuffield Foundation and Joseph Rowntree Foundation
  • Charities and public health bodies conducting programme research
What you could do now

Data and Analytics

Data and analytics roles are one of the fastest-growing areas of graduate employment. Psychology graduates bring a distinctive combination of quantitative skills and understanding of human behaviour that sets them apart in this space.

Why Psychology?
  • Statistical training in psychology — including inferential statistics, regression, and data visualisation — directly underpins data analyst roles
  • Experience with data analysis software such as SPSS, R, or Excel is directly transferable
  • Understanding of research methodology means psychology graduates can critically evaluate data and avoid common analytical errors
  • Knowledge of human behaviour adds interpretive depth to data analysis that purely technical graduates may lack
  • Skills in communicating complex findings to non-specialist audiences are highly valued by employers
Possible roles in this area
Data Analyst
Collects, processes, and interprets large datasets to generate insights that inform business or organisational decisions.
Graduate entry
Consumer Insights Analyst
Applies research and analytical skills to understand how and why consumers make decisions, informing marketing and product strategy.
Graduate entry
UX Researcher
Plans and conducts research to understand how users interact with products and services, using both quantitative and qualitative methods.
Graduate entry
People Analytics Analyst
Uses data to understand workforce trends, employee wellbeing, and organisational effectiveness — a growing area within HR.
Graduate entry
Skills that may be useful
Statistics and data analysis SPSS, R or Excel Data visualisation Critical thinking Communication of findings Attention to detail
Example employers
  • Technology companies and digital agencies
  • Market research firms including YouGov, Ipsos, and Kantar
  • Financial services, retail, and consumer goods companies
  • NHS and public sector bodies with data and intelligence teams
What you could do now
  • Go beyond what is required in your statistics modules — build confidence with SPSS, R, or Excel
  • Use free datasets from the UK Data Service or Kaggle to practise analysis and build a portfolio
  • Consider free online courses in data analysis, SQL, or Python to supplement your degree
  • Look into graduate analyst schemes at large employers — many are open to any degree subject

Evaluation and Impact

Evaluation and impact roles are a growing area, particularly in the charity, public health, and public sector fields. They draw directly on psychology research skills to assess whether programmes and interventions are working, and communicate findings to inform future decisions.

Why Psychology?
  • Research methods training provides the foundation for designing and conducting programme evaluations
  • Knowledge of psychological theory helps evaluators understand why interventions do or do not work
  • Skills in both quantitative and qualitative data collection and analysis are directly applicable
  • Understanding of behaviour change theory is particularly relevant in health and social care evaluation
  • Ability to communicate complex findings clearly to commissioners, funders, and the public
Possible roles in this area
Evaluation Officer
Assesses the effectiveness of programmes and interventions using a range of research methods, and reports findings to internal and external stakeholders.
Graduate entry
Monitoring and Evaluation Officer
Tracks and reports on programme delivery and outcomes, often working in charities, local authorities, or international development organisations.
Graduate entry
Impact Analyst
Measures and communicates the social or commercial impact of an organisation's programmes, often producing annual impact reports or case studies.
Graduate entry
Skills that may be useful
Research methods Data collection and analysis Report writing Stakeholder communication Critical thinking Project management
Example employers
  • Charities and third sector organisations with impact and evaluation functions
  • NHS and public health bodies evaluating health programmes
  • Local authorities and government departments
  • Evaluation consultancies and social research firms
What you could do now
  • Volunteer with a charity and offer to help with data collection or reporting — many small organisations need this support
  • Build familiarity with logic models and theory of change frameworks, which are widely used in evaluation
  • Look for graduate research or evaluation roles in charities and public health organisations
  • Use your dissertation to develop a strong example of applying research methods to a real question
