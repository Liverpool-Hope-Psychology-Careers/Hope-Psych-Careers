Sport and Exercise

Sport psychology, performance coaching and exercise science — careers supporting athletes and promoting wellbeing through physical activity.

If you are interested in sport, physical activity, health, motivation, and performance, there are a range of careers where psychology can be applied. This area includes roles in performance support, athlete welfare, health promotion, and, with further training, sport psychology practice.

What you could do now
  • Volunteer in sports clubs or community sport settings
  • Gain experience in welfare or support roles
  • Work with university sport teams
  • Build communication and helping skills through peer mentoring
  • Look into BPS student membership and Division of Sport and Exercise Psychology events

Sport Performance

Sport performance roles focus on helping athletes perform at their best while also supporting motivation, wellbeing, and life balance.

Why Psychology?
  • Understanding of motivation, behaviour, and what drives performance
  • Communication and rapport-building skills developed through study and placements
  • Knowledge of wellbeing, stress, and mental health in high-pressure environments
  • Research and data analysis skills applicable to performance analysis
  • Understanding of group dynamics and team behaviour
Possible roles in this area
Athlete Welfare Officer
Supports the psychological and personal wellbeing of athletes within sport organisations, providing safeguarding and pastoral support.
Graduate entry
Performance Lifestyle Adviser
Helps athletes manage the demands of elite sport alongside education, career planning, and everyday life.
Graduate entry
Performance Analyst
Collects and analyses data on athlete and team performance to inform coaching decisions and training programmes.
Graduate entry
Skills that may be useful
Communication Empathy Motivation and behaviour Wellbeing support Data analysis Professional boundaries
Example employers
  • English Institute of Sport
  • British Cycling, British Athletics and other national governing bodies
  • Premier League and professional sports clubs
  • Universities and higher education sport departments
What you could do now
  • Volunteer with sports clubs or university teams
  • Gain experience in welfare or pastoral support roles
  • Build data analysis skills through online courses
  • Attend BPS Division of Sport and Exercise Psychology student events

Health and Physical Activity

Health and physical activity roles promote exercise and healthy behaviour in community and clinical settings, drawing on behaviour change theory and public health knowledge.

Why Psychology?
  • Deep understanding of behaviour change theory and how to apply it in practice
  • Knowledge of motivation, habit formation, and what helps people sustain healthy behaviours
  • Skills in designing and evaluating programmes and interventions
  • Person-centred communication skills, including motivational interviewing
  • Understanding of health inequalities and the social determinants of health
Possible roles in this area
Health Promotion Officer
Designs and delivers programmes and campaigns encouraging people to be more physically active and make healthier choices.
Graduate entry
Exercise Referral Specialist
Works with patients referred by GPs to use physical activity as a therapeutic intervention for long-term health conditions.
Specialist training needed
Skills that may be useful
Behaviour change knowledge Community engagement Programme design Evaluation Person-centred communication Motivational interviewing
Example employers
  • NHS Trusts and GP surgeries
  • Local authorities and Active Partnerships
  • Charities focused on health and physical activity
  • Public Health England and regional public health teams
What you could do now
  • Volunteer in community health or physical activity programmes
  • Look into NHS volunteering opportunities
  • Explore behaviour change modules in your degree
  • Research public health graduate schemes

Sport Psychology

Sport psychology careers require postgraduate training beyond a psychology undergraduate degree, leading to chartered or registered practitioner status. These are highly rewarding roles for those passionate about combining psychology and sport.

Why Psychology?
  • In-depth knowledge of psychological theory and evidence-based intervention
  • Research and evaluation skills essential for practice and academic work
  • Strong foundations in understanding behaviour, motivation, and performance under pressure
  • Understanding of mental health and wellbeing in competitive environments
  • Ethical awareness and professional practice skills developed throughout the degree
Possible roles in this area
Sport Psychologist
Works with athletes and teams to optimise performance and support mental health using evidence-based psychological interventions such as goal-setting, imagery, and cognitive techniques.
Postgrad training required
Chartered Sport and Exercise Psychologist
A fully qualified and HCPC-registered practitioner who provides psychological support to athletes, coaches, and organisations at all levels of sport.
Postgrad training required
Academic Researcher in Sport Psychology
Conducts research into psychological aspects of sport and exercise performance, typically based in a university setting and requiring a PhD.
PhD required
Skills that may be useful
Psychological theory Research and evaluation Rapport building Ethical practice Working under pressure Intervention design
Example employers
  • National sport governing bodies and institutes of sport
  • Professional sports clubs and academies
  • Universities (research and teaching roles)
  • Private practice and independent consultancy
What you could do now
  • Research BPS-accredited MSc programmes in Sport and Exercise Psychology
  • Gain experience working with athletes or in sport settings
  • Attend BPS Division of Sport and Exercise Psychology events
  • Consider volunteering in performance or welfare roles to strengthen your application
  • Talk to academic staff about undergraduate research opportunities in sport psychology
