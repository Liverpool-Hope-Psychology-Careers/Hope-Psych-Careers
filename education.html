Education

Teaching, educational psychology, learning support and schools — careers where psychological knowledge improves outcomes for children and young people.

Psychology graduates bring valuable understanding of learning, development, and behaviour to a range of education-related careers. Whether in the classroom, supporting students with additional needs, guiding young people through career decisions, or working in higher education, the knowledge and skills developed through a psychology degree are directly applicable.

What you could do now
  • Volunteer as a peer mentor or student ambassador to build communication and support skills
  • Look into school experience placements through Get Into Teaching
  • Apply for a teaching assistant or learning support role as part-time work
  • Explore the widening participation and outreach activities run by Liverpool Hope
  • Consider whether a PGCE or School Direct route into teaching might be right for you

School-based Roles

Schools offer a range of roles for psychology graduates, from classroom teaching to mentoring and support, where understanding of child development, learning, and behaviour is directly relevant.

Why Psychology?
  • Understanding of child and adolescent development provides a strong foundation for working with young people
  • Knowledge of learning theory — how people acquire, retain and apply knowledge — is directly applicable to teaching
  • Understanding of behaviour, motivation, and what helps young people engage with learning
  • Communication and interpersonal skills developed throughout the psychology degree
  • Awareness of mental health and wellbeing in young people, increasingly valued in school settings
Possible roles in this area
Teacher (with PGCE)
Qualified classroom teachers, with psychology graduates particularly suited to PSHE, social sciences, and subjects involving human behaviour. Requires a PGCE or School Direct qualification.
PGCE required
Teaching Assistant
Provides classroom support to teachers and pupils, often with a focus on students with additional learning needs or SEND. A widely accessible graduate entry role.
Graduate entry
Learning Mentor
Supports students with academic, social, or emotional challenges in school settings, drawing on understanding of wellbeing, motivation, and behaviour.
Graduate entry
SENCO / SEND Support
Coordinates support for students with special educational needs and disabilities. SENCO roles typically require teaching experience, but SEND support roles are accessible as a graduate.
Graduate entry
Skills that may be useful
Communication Empathy and patience Child development knowledge Behaviour management Differentiation and inclusion Organisation
Example employers
  • Primary and secondary schools across the maintained and independent sectors
  • Special educational needs schools and specialist provisions
  • Alternative provision settings and pupil referral units
  • Multi-academy trusts running groups of schools
What you could do now
  • Explore school experience through Get Into Teaching — free placements available
  • Apply for a teaching assistant role as part-time or voluntary work
  • Research PGCE and School Direct routes, including subject-specific options
  • Look into tuition and mentoring opportunities for local young people

Educational Support

Beyond classroom teaching, psychology graduates can pursue a range of roles focused on student welfare, access to education, and career guidance — areas where psychological understanding is directly valuable.

Why Psychology?
  • Understanding of barriers to learning — including mental health, socioeconomic factors, and motivation — informs effective support
  • Knowledge of adolescent development and the challenges facing young people in education
  • Strong communication and rapport-building skills for working with students and families
  • Research skills for evaluating the effectiveness of interventions and support programmes
  • Awareness of safeguarding, confidentiality, and professional ethics in education settings
Possible roles in this area
Careers Adviser
Helps students explore career options and make informed decisions about their future, drawing on understanding of motivation, identity, and decision-making.
Graduate entry
Education Welfare Officer
Works with schools and families to support student attendance and wellbeing, often involving casework with vulnerable young people and their families.
Graduate entry
Widening Participation Officer
Supports underrepresented students in accessing and succeeding in higher education, working in outreach, mentoring, and programme delivery.
Graduate entry
Education Outreach Officer
Engages with schools and communities to promote educational opportunities and raise aspirations, often working for universities or charities.
Graduate entry
Skills that may be useful
Communication and listening Empathy Understanding of barriers to education Casework and record keeping Programme planning Partnership working
Example employers
  • Universities and higher education institutions — widening participation teams
  • Local authorities — education welfare and SEND teams
  • Schools and multi-academy trusts
  • Charities focused on education access and youth support
What you could do now
  • Get involved in Liverpool Hope's widening participation activities as a student ambassador
  • Volunteer with youth organisations or mentoring programmes
  • Research the Career Development Institute and routes into careers guidance
  • Look for education support roles in local schools or charities

Higher Education

Universities employ psychology graduates in both academic and professional services roles. From student support and widening participation to research and lecturing, higher education offers a wide range of career paths.

Why Psychology?
  • Research and analytical skills underpin both academic lecturing and evidence-based professional services work
  • Understanding of student wellbeing, mental health, and the challenges of the student experience
  • Communication and presentation skills developed throughout the degree
  • Knowledge of diversity, inclusion, and access — increasingly important in HE professional services
  • For academic roles: strong foundation for postgraduate study and original research
Possible roles in this area
Student Support Adviser
Provides pastoral and practical support to university students, drawing on understanding of wellbeing, mental health, and the challenges of student life.
Graduate entry
Widening Participation / Outreach Officer
Works to improve access and success for underrepresented groups in higher education, through outreach, mentoring, and programme delivery.
Graduate entry
Disability and Inclusion Adviser
Supports students with disabilities, mental health conditions, or additional needs to access and succeed in higher education.
Graduate entry
Academic Lecturer / Researcher
Teaches and conducts research at university level. Typically requires a PhD and a developing publication record. Psychology graduates are well positioned for postgraduate study.
PhD required
Skills that may be useful
Communication Empathy and active listening Understanding of mental health Research skills Inclusion and diversity awareness Organisation
Example employers
  • Universities and higher education institutions across the UK
  • Students' Unions and student services departments
  • Research institutes and postgraduate programmes
  • Widening participation and access programmes including NCOP
What you could do now
  • Explore student-facing roles at Liverpool Hope — student ambassadors, peer mentors, and student reps all build relevant skills
  • If interested in academic careers, speak to staff about research opportunities and postgraduate routes
  • Look for graduate roles in university student services, disability teams, or widening participation
  • Research ESRC and other postgraduate funding routes if you are considering a PhD
